How it works

Three jobs. Human-first. Agent-native.

01

Connect once

Agent Ledger plugs into agent banking APIs over MCP, or you drop in a CSV. Works with Meow, Bank of Bots, and any CSV-exportable ledger.

02

Group by agent, not by card

Every transaction gets tagged to the agent that ran it. You see "ResearchBot spent $47 today" instead of "Virtual card ending 4421 had 12 charges."

03

Flag the weird stuff

Duplicate charges, spend velocity spikes, unusual vendors, off-hours bursts. Rules you can tune. A simple summary for the 5pm check-in.

FAQ

Short answers, no hedging.

Why not just look at my bank dashboard?
Bank dashboards group by card and time. You want to group by agent and intent. "Virtual card 4421 charged $4.99 at perplexity.ai" means nothing. "ResearchBot ran the same query 14 times in 3 minutes" is the signal.
How is this different from Meow's built-in audit log?
Meow logs every transaction — good. It doesn't cluster by agent, surface anomalies, or cross-reference the agent's task log to answer "why." That's the gap. Agent Ledger sits on top of whatever Meow or Bank of Bots exposes.
Is this just AppZen for agents?
AppZen audits human expense reports. Agents don't file expense reports — they move money directly. The data model is different: agents have task IDs, prompt logs, and tool-call chains that humans don't. Agent Ledger is built around those, not receipts.
What's the pricing?
Free for the first 100 teams through beta. After that, likely a per-agent monthly price with a free read-only tier. Teams with compliance requirements will get a separate SOC-2-ready plan.
Can I self-host?
Yes — the audit engine is a small set of rules over your own agent logs. If you want to run it in your VPC, tell me in the waitlist note and I'll prioritize a Docker image.
